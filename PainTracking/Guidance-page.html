Pain Symptoms Guide

Understanding pain symptoms and their management can help improve the well-being. Below are detailed guides on common pain symptoms, their causes, and steps you can take to manage them effectively to see your improvement of tracking your pain symptoms.

Guidance on tracking the pain symptoms

Pain Severity (0-10): 0 - No Pain and 10 - Pain. It is a way to describe the pain level and journey of the start, to how it is going so far.

Duration (minutes):The duration is a way to measure how long the pain lasts during each episode, in minutes. This helps track the persistence of the pain. A high severity score with a short duration may indicate a different type of issue than a low severity score with a long duration. It helps in understanding the impact of the pain over time.

Location of Pain: It is to specify the area of the body where the pain is felt. Users can choose from a list of common locations, or it could be adapted to include more specific areas, depending on the condition being tracked (e.g., chest, back, joints).

Type of Pain:This describes the quality of the pain (e.g., sharp, dull, throbbing, pressure). This helps capture the characteristics of the pain experience and provides further details about the pain's nature. Understanding the type of pain can help differentiate between various conditions. For instance, a sharp pain might suggest an injury or nerve issue, while a dull pain could indicate muscle strain or stress. Knowing the type of pain can lead to better treatment or management plans.

Potential Trigger:To identify what might have caused or exacerbated the pain. The user can specify triggers like stress, physical activity and certain foods. Identifying potential triggers helps in managing the pain. If a user can recognize that a certain trigger (like stress or exercise) is causing or increasing pain, they can take preventive measures to avoid it.

How to view pain records?

To view your previous pain records:

  • Go to the Dashboard in order to track and you can see the History section an example of it from the sidebar menu of the HomePage.
  • Select the specific pain type (e.g., Headache, Back Pain) to view related entries.
  • You can see a list of information on pain symptoms with details like date, severity, and duration.
  • Takes you back to the Dashboard depending which pain symptom you tracked, you click on it then you can view the records.

How to delete pain entries?

To remove a pain log entry:

  • Go to the Dashboard page.
  • Find the entry you want to delete, it can be found in the records which in it are listed by pain type and date.
  • Click the Delete button next to that entry to remove it from the records.
  • You'll be asked to confirm before the entry is deleted.
